Year 3, #9

Saturday, 24 April 2004
Laweyan, Solo

Dear Friends,

Last night we had the 9th klenengan of the 3rd fiscal year of the North-American-sponsored klenengan. It started at 8:00 pm and ended at 2:00 am. This klenengan had 2 distinct phases—quite serious and staid in feeling all the way through 11:30, then quite light and lively until 2 am. Usually things get a little lighter around 10 pm or so, but this month there was a longer list of gendhing pre-planned by Pak Wahyo and Pak Dalimin. However, Akira from Japan, who has been in Solo for over 2 years, was leaving to go home in the morning, so at 11:30 some “goodbye” jineman and langgam for him started. Also there was a special performance at SriWedari, which ended at 11:00, resulting in an influx of excited—a sponsor from Jakarta had given them all some extra pocket money—and slightly intoxicated wayang orang musicians. Mas Darsono “Jepang” also arrived around 10:30. (Need I say more...) Both parts of the klenengan were a success, with different musicians shining at different moments. Again, very typical of most klenengan, but particularly stark and sudden in contrast last night. Like the whole feeling just turned on a coin as Roning Gadhung ciblon was coming to an end.

We had a guest rebab player from Jakarta, Bp. Hartono. He is an old friend of both Pak Wakidjo’s (a former neighbor) and Pak Harjito’s (SMKI) from the 1960’s in Solo, and a regular in the group that rehearses at our house in Jakarta.

Ibu Tarini has been absent for 3 klenengan now—she finally had an operation to remove long-time cysts she’s had, and is still recovering in the hospital. Bu Tukinem is doing very well after her sinus operation, but not well enough to go out until 2 am and sing. There was a rehearsal at Bp. Bambang Suwarno’s house for a dhalang competition, so many of the younger musicians couldn’t attend. Sekaten starts tomorrow at the Kraton, and Pak Anom/Bayu were performing as well, so a few of the musicians were absent with those responsibilities.

The next klenengan will be on Saturday, 22 May at Laweyan.
