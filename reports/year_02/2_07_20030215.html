Year 2, #7

Monday, 1 January 2001
Laweyan, Solo

Dear Friends,

Last Saturday night we had the 7th klenengan of the 2nd fiscal year at Laweyan in Solo. It started at 8:00 pm and ended at 2:10 am. The klenengan was very, very full and quite lively, especially during the last few pieces when the sake kicked in that was brought by a group of Japanese students who came with Bp. Saptono.

We had a sadder and more sober moment at the very end when we announced to the musicians the passing of Anne Stebingerís father last Wednesday. Arnold Stebinger passed away on Wednesday after suffering from Parkinsonís disease for 2 years. He and Jean Stebinger were the founders of the original malam Minggu Wage klenengan, which ran for 7 months in 1999-2000 in Badran, Solo and was the inspiration for this more extensive klenengan. The musicians expressed their high regard for his generosity, as well as extending their deepest condolences to Anne and Jean Stebinger.

There werenít as many foreigners listening this month for a surprising reasonóthe e-mail I routinely send to the gamelan list announcing the klenengan about 5 days before somehow didnít get sent correctly and didnít make it through. It turns out that the foreigners in Solo depend on that e-mail more than anything they hear from local musicians or just counting Saturdays on the calendar! None of the regular students showed up except Akira, who Iíd run into the night before, and in only one case was it due to another conflict. Amazing how the mechanism for getting news of an upcoming klenengan in Solo has changed in the past decade!
